As a kinesthetic learner -- a learner who learns more by doing, the only way I can learn and do my goals are doing it.

In mathematics, I got started in arithmetic. I watched Udacity's Intro to Algebra Review and College Algebra's first part of the lesson and answered the exercises. It's focused on numbers, operators and properties (associative and commutative).

In Computing, I plan to get started in the C programming language with the ANSI standard. I found a book called "The C Programming Language (Second Edition) [ANSI C]", a book written by its creators -- Dennis Ritchie and Brian Kerningham.

I haven't got started in Science and the English Language but I haven't found a course for the English Language but found a course called "Intro to Physics" for Science.

As for being physically fit, I am to exercise with intervals incrementing by one each period.

You might think that this is too much for me but as I said, it doesn't have a time limit. There is an idiom that motivates me from doing these which is:

"Jack of all trades, master of some,
Better than Jack of one trade, master of one."
